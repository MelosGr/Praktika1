    • Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC)

    Post-Quantum Cryptography refers to cryptographic techniques that are resistant to attacks from both classical and quantum computers. The development of PQC algorithms aims to future-proof encryption, ensuring that data remains secure even in the presence of fully operational quantum machines.

    Over the past few years, researchers, companies, and governments have been working to develop and standardize PQC algorithms. In 2016, the U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) launched a global competition to identify the best PQC algorithms. After multiple rounds of evaluation, NIST announced its first batch of selected algorithms in 2022, including CRYSTALS-Kyber and CRYSTALS-Dilithium, which focus on public-key encryption and digital signatures, respectively.

    As the world edges closer to the advent of quantum computing, a powerful new technology with the potential to revolutionize industries, security experts are racing against the clock to safeguard our digital world from the very real threat it poses: the breaking of classical encryption methods. This new era of cryptography, known as Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC), is designed to protect sensitive data from the capabilities of quantum computers.
